I am a Research Associate at the **Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Ropar**, specializing in low-dimensional topology and knot theory. My research primarily focuses on non-classical knotting phenomena, specifically exploring the mathematical structures of **twisted, welded, virtual, and surface knot theories**. 

Currently, my work focuses on developing and computing novel **diagrammatic invariants** for twisted knots to better classify and understand their topological properties. 

## Research Interests
* **Non-Classical Knot Theories:** Exploring structural properties of twisted, virtual, and welded knots and links.
* **Knot Invariants:** Constructing diagrammatic invariants and investigating $s$-invariants within combinatorial and homological frameworks.
* **Surface Knots:** Studying the embeddings of surfaces in higher-dimensional spaces and their diagrammatic representations.

## Current Projects
### Diagrammatic Invariants of Twisted Knots
Developing systematic algebraic and combinatorial tools to distinguish non-equivalent twisted knots. This involves analyzing their Gauss diagrams and defining state-sum or polynomial invariants specific to the twisted framework.

### $s$-Invariants and Slice Invariants
Investigating extensions of Khovanov homology-related invariants, like Rasmussen's $s$-invariant, to virtual and twisted settings to explore the slice genus of these configurations.
